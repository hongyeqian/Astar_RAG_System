## **Transcript Summary**



In this interview, martial arts legend **Jackie Chan** discussed his career, his honorary **Lifetime Achievement Oscar**, and his desire for serious dramatic roles. Chan expressed surprise and deep honor at being the first Chinese actor to receive the Academy Award. A central part of the conversation was the physical sacrifice of his career, as he used a body chart to detail numerous **broken bones** and **injuries** across his head, jaw, and limbs. He voiced his frustration with Hollywood for continually typecasting him in **"action comedy"** roles, specifically as a police or CIA agent, while stating his ambition is to take on roles in romantic or dramatic films, such as running in slow motion on a beach and kissing a girl. He promoted his new film, **The Foreigner**, which provides an opportunity to play a more serious, 70-year-old character with a specialized background.

------



## **Key Topics**





### **1. Career Milestone: Honorary Oscar**



- Chan received an honorary Oscar for his **Lifetime Achievement** in film, noting he was the first Chinese actor to do so.
- He described the experience of being informed as an unexpected surprise.



### **2. Physical Toll and Stunts**



- Chan confirmed he still performs his own stunts, maintaining the audience expectation set by his "old, traditional" movies.
- He detailed a long list of injuries, including a **hole in his head**, **broken jaw, cheek,** and numerous other breaks and cracks across his body.
- The chart illustrated the extensive physical sacrifice required by his action career.



### **3. Typecasting and Role Ambition**



- Chan expressed frustration with being consistently typecast in the U.S. as a **police officer or CIA agent** in **action comedies**.
- He voiced a desire for roles in different genres, specifically mentioning **dramas** and **romantic films** (e.g., *La La Land* or *Meet the Parents*).
- His unfulfilled ambition includes running on the beach, singing, and kissing a girl in slow motion, a role no director has hired him for.



### **4. New Film: The Foreigner**



- The film is highlighted as a departure, showing a **more serious side** and a fighting style that is less comedic.
- Chan plays an older character (described as being "like a 70 years old" guy) with a special background, allowing for a **more dramatic performance**.
