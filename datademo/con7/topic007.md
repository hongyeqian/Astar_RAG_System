## The Recommendation "Discovery Engine"

- TikTok’s algorithm functions as a “discovery engine” built on user interests, not social connections.
- The system learns through user actions (likes, watch time, shares) to predict what content each person will enjoy.
- Chew emphasized that the algorithm is “just math,” optimizing for engagement signals.
- The design enables creators like Khaby Lame to gain visibility without prior followers.
- The platform’s goal is to “inspire creativity and bring joy” by giving everyone a fair chance to be discovered. 

## Addiction and Screen Time Management

- Chew said TikTok aims for a healthy long-term relationship with users rather than maximizing screen time.
- The app shows reminders encouraging users to take breaks after extended viewing.
- Teens under 18 have a default 60-minute daily limit, following recommendations from Boston Children’s Hospital.
- Parents can use the “Family Pairing” feature to set limits and monitor activity.
- These controls reflect a shift toward well-being and digital balance for young users. 

## Content Moderation and Safety

- TikTok enforces clear Community Guidelines against violence, pornography, and harmful challenges.
- A mix of human moderators and AI systems—numbering in the tens of thousands—reviews content continuously.
- Younger users experience stricter protections: no DMs under 16 and no livestreaming under 18.
- Chew highlighted efforts to filter out sexualized or mature themes from teenage feeds.
- The approach combines rule enforcement, technology, and differentiated youth experiences. 

## Data, China, and "Project Texas"

- U.S. data concerns were addressed through “Project Texas,” a plan to localize data on U.S. soil.
- All new American user data is stored on Oracle Cloud servers, managed by U.S. personnel.
- Older data from Virginia and Singapore servers is being deleted as part of the transition.
- Chew called Project Texas “an unprecedented project” for transparency and compliance.
- The initiative aims to isolate U.S. data from ByteDance and eliminate government access risk. 

## Election Integrity and Transparency

- Chew pledged that TikTok would remain free from government manipulation or propaganda.
- TikTok offers third-party auditing of its source code for external verification.
- Anderson questioned the potential for election interference; Chew responded with commitments to openness.
- Transparency is the core defense mechanism for building public and governmental trust.
- Chew reaffirmed that no government, including China’s, can influence TikTok’s U.S. operations.
